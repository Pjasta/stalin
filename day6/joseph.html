The Indian Army is the land-based branch and largest component of the Indian Armed Forces, with a primary mission of ensuring national security, defending against external and internal threats, and maintaining peace. It is one of the largest standing armies globally, renowned for its bravery, discipline, and humanitarian assistance. The Indian Army is highly structured, operating operationally and geographically across seven regional and training commands.Commands: There are 6 operational commands and 1 training command, each headed by a Lieutenant General.Hierarchy: The forces are generally broken down into Corps, Divisions, Brigades, and Battalions.Ranks: The personnel hierarchy spans from Commissioned Officers (leaders and decision-makers), Junior Commissioned Officers (field managers), to Non-Commissioned Officers and Jawans (operational soldiers).
